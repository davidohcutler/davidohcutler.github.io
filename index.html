About me

Hi! I’m David (Owen Horace) Cutler, an undergraduate studying mathematics at Tufts University. I enjoy geometry: much of my research thus far has revolved either around metric-geometric problems emerging from complex geometry, or around existence problems within complex geometry concerning metrics with prescribed behavior.

I write sometimes on problems in recreational mathematics, and have contributed some simpler combinatorial work to the OEIS.

Outside of math, I hike, write poetry, and listen to a lot of music. I’m also a very big fan of cats — you can see mine below!
